Alzheimer’s Disease

Alzheimer’s disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects millions of individuals worldwide. It not only impacts the person diagnosed but also their loved ones who become caregivers on this challenging journey. Understanding Alzheimer’s disease, its symptoms, available treatments, and avenues for support is crucial in providing compassionate care and improving quality of life for those affected.

Symptoms of Alzheimer’s Disease

Alzheimer’s disease typically manifests in gradual cognitive decline, affecting memory, thinking, and behavior. Common symptoms include:

  • Memory loss, especially recent events or conversations.
  • Difficulty in problem-solving, planning, or completing familiar tasks.
  • Confusion about time or place.
  • Challenges with language, such as finding the right words or following a conversation.
  • Changes in mood or personality, including withdrawal from social activities.
